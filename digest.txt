Claude Code 现已支持计算机操作功能，Claude 可以直接在 CLI 中打开应用、操作界面并测试自己生成的代码。该功能目前以研究预览版形式向 Pro 和 Max 计划用户开放。

🎙 Podcast

Building Is the Easy Part Now | Mike Krieger on What AI Changed

Instagram 联合创始人、现任 Anthropic Labs 产品负责人 Mike Krieger 指出，尽管 AI 已将软件构建本身变得极为轻松——他用 Claude 仅花两小时就重建了 Instagram 前身 Bourbon 的完整功能——但培养产品直觉这一更核心的工作，仍然需要时间积累与真实世界的磨砺。最令人意外的观察是：AI 模型擅长添加功能，却根本不擅长判断该删除什么，导致 AI 加速开发往往产出功能臃肿、缺乏深层判断力的通用产品。他借用两个比喻来阐释这一问题：室内生长、从未经历风吹的树木结构不够强韧；以及被直接丢进电视剧大结局却毫无前情铺垫的观看体验——两者都指向同一个道理：跳过成长阵痛所得到的成果，外表完整却内里空洞。他的核心论断是：AI 时代鲜有爆款消费产品出现，根本原因或许并非构建难度，而是缺乏只有在更慢、更艰难的经历中才能锻造出的产品信念。

Mike Krieger, Instagram co-founder and Anthropic Labs product leader, argues that while AI has made the *building* of software trivially fast—he rebuilt the entire pre-Instagram app Bourbon in two hours using Claude—the harder, irreplaceable work of developing product intuition still requires time and real-world friction. Most surprisingly, he observes that AI models are good at adding features but fundamentally poor at knowing what to cut, meaning AI-accelerated development tends to produce overbuilt, generic products lacking the deep editorial judgment that comes from incremental, user-exposed iteration. He draws on two metaphors: a tree grown indoors without wind that never develops structural strength, and being dropped into a TV finale with no prior character context—both illustrating how skipping the formative struggle produces something that looks complete but isn't truly sound. His broader provocation is that the scarcity of breakout consumer products in the AI era may be explained not by building difficulty, but by a shortage of earned product conviction that only slower, harder experience can forge.
