① Andrej Karpathy · AI Researcher & Educator

Karpathy 正在探索 LLM 的新用途：用它来构建个人知识库，让 LLM 不只是处理代码，而是迭代地整理和存储结构化知识。他进一步设想，未来向前沿 LLM 提一个问题，就能自动召唤一组 LLM 协作，生成一个临时 wiki、进行校验，最终输出完整报告。

---

Karpathy is exploring a new use case for LLMs: building personal knowledge bases on research topics, where LLMs iteratively construct and refine structured knowledge rather than just manipulating code. He envisions a natural extension where a single query to a frontier LLM spawns an entire team of LLMs that builds an ephemeral wiki, lints it, and produces a full report — far beyond simple text decoding.

🎙 Podcast

How Autonomous Labs Will Transform Scientific Research: Ginkgo Bioworks’ Jason Kelly

Ginkgo Bioworks创始人兼CEO杰森·凯利表示，AI是三十年来首个真正从根本上变革生物技术的技术革命——不同于互联网或社交媒体，他将后者对生物技术的影响斥为"后台IT杂务"。公司创立于2008年，艰难自力更生六年后，经山姆·奥特曼牵线加入YC才获得资本支持。Ginkgo早期以"生物铸造厂"为核心产品，致力于将DNA编辑的编译调试周期自动化、规模化。凯利透露了一项重要战略转型：面对Chai Bio、Arc Institute等机构在AI生物设计领域的激烈竞争，Ginkgo已将重心完全转向降低实验室实验的时间与成本，并通过与OpenAI合作探索AI自主驱动实验室流程的可能性——而这一方向在传统生命科学工具企业中仍几乎无人涉足。

Ginkgo Bioworks CEO Jason Kelly argues that AI represents the first technology revolution in 30 years to fundamentally transform biotechnology — unlike the internet or social media, which he dismisses as "back office IT crap" for the industry. Founded in 2008 and bootstrapped for six years before a pivotal YC introduction via Sam Altman, Ginkgo pioneered the concept of "biological foundries" — centralized, automated labs that compress the compile-debug cycle of DNA engineering. Kelly reveals a significant strategic pivot: Ginkgo has stepped back from AI-driven biological design (now crowded with players like Chai Bio and Arc Institute's EVO2) to focus exclusively on making physical lab experimentation faster and cheaper. The company is exploring how AI agents — including through a collaboration with OpenAI — can autonomously operate laboratory workflows, a space Kelly believes remains largely unaddressed by traditional life science tools vendors who lack Silicon Valley's disruptive mindset.
