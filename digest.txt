🎙 Podcast

Marc Andreessen introspects on The Death of the Browser, Pi + OpenClaw, and Why &quot;This Time Is Different&quot;

马克·安德森在 a16z 的原始办公室接受采访时提出，当今的 AI 突破本质上是"八十年铸就的一夜成名"——数十年的基础研究积累在同一时刻集中爆发，仅神经网络架构这一概念就耗费了六七十年才被学界广泛认可。他否认了外界关于 a16z 错过 AI 浪潮的说法，强调自己从1980年代末便深度参与 AI 研究，包括在专家系统热潮期间使用 Lisp 编程。他将 2013 年的 AlexNet 和 2017 年的 Transformer 视为真正的技术拐点，并揭示了一个鲜为人知的细节：谷歌等大公司早已拥有可用的内部聊天机器人，却刻意对公众封锁。此外，对话还提到一个有趣的历史插曲——AI Dungeon 游戏曾一度是普通用户与 GPT-3 交互的唯一公开渠道。

Marc Andreessen, speaking at a16z's original office, argues that today's AI breakthroughs represent an "80-year overnight success" — decades of foundational research suddenly unlocking at scale, with the neural network architecture alone taking 60-70 years to be accepted as definitively correct. He pushes back on the narrative that a16z missed the AI wave, noting personal involvement in AI since the late 1980s including coding in Lisp during the original expert systems boom. He identifies AlexNet (2013) and the Transformer (2017) as the true inflection points, and highlights a surprisingly long suppression period where major companies like Google had working internal chatbots but deliberately withheld them from the public. The conversation also touches on the quirky historical footnote that AI Dungeon was briefly the only public interface through which ordinary users could interact with GPT-3.
