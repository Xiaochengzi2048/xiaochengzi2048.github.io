Former Tesla/OpenAI AI Director Andrej Karpathy flagged a subtle but important UX problem with LLM personalization: memory features are actively distracting models. When a single unrelated fact (e.g. "I like cats") is stored, it tends to bleed into every response — and this pattern appears across all major LLMs, not just one implementation. His observation: "A single question could be the memory about 'I like cats' and suddenly the AI is putting cats in every response." This suggests the industry still hasn't solved contextual relevance in personalization.
https://x.com/karpathy/status/2036841069636370467
https://x.com/karpathy/status/2036836816654147718

Andrej Karpathy（前 Tesla/OpenAI AI 总监）指出了 LLM 个性化中一个微妙但重要的 UX 问题：记忆功能正在干扰模型。当一个不相关的事实（比如"我喜欢猫"）被存储后，它往往会渗透到每一条回复中——而且这个现象几乎出现在所有主流 LLM 上，并非某个具体实现的问题。这说明整个行业在个性化中的"上下文相关性"问题上仍未解决。

🎙 Training Data — "Biology's Waymo Moment: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly"
https://youtube.com/watch?v=g45Alfg7diw

**The Takeaway:** AI is finally doing something that no previous tech wave could — it's becoming load-bearing infrastructure for biology and pharma, not just back-office IT.

Ginkgo Bioworks CEO Jason Kelly makes a striking argument: every major tech revolution before AI — the internet, social media, mobile — was essentially irrelevant to biotech and biopharma. "Yeah it's nice, we communicate slightly better, whatever — it's just some back-office IT crap." AI is different. For the first time, software is directly interfacing with the core work of biology: reading genomes, designing proteins, automating lab workflows.

Kelly draws a direct parallel to autonomous vehicles. Just as Waymo spent years building the underlying tech stack before meaningful deployment, biology is now at its own "Waymo moment" — the infrastructure is reaching the threshold where real-world application becomes viable at scale. The bottleneck shifts from "can we do this?" to "how fast can we deploy?"

What makes this compelling for builders: Ginkgo isn't just a biotech company, it's a platform play. They're building the cell programming infrastructure that other companies can build on top of — an AWS analogy for synthetic biology. Kelly sees AI as the unlock that finally makes this platform economically viable, because it dramatically reduces the cost of the design-build-test cycle that is the core loop of all biology work.

The counterintuitive insight: the reason prior tech didn't matter to biotech was that the bottleneck was never communication or information management — it was the ability to actually design and manipulate biological systems. AI addresses exactly that bottleneck.

---

**要点：** AI 终于做到了以前任何技术浪潮都无法做到的事——它正在成为生物学和医药领域真正的基础设施，而不仅仅是后台 IT 工具。

Ginkgo Bioworks CEO Jason Kelly 提出了一个令人印象深刻的论点：在 AI 之前，每一次重大技术革命——互联网、社交媒体、移动互联网——对生物科技和生物制药来说基本上都是无关紧要的。"是挺好的，我们沟通稍微顺畅一点，但就这样——不过是些后台 IT 的小把戏。"AI 不同。这是第一次，软件直接介入生物学的核心工作：读取基因组、设计蛋白质、自动化实验室流程。

Kelly 直接类比了自动驾驶汽车。就像 Waymo 花了多年时间打造底层技术栈，才得以真正部署，生物学现在正处于自己的"Waymo 时刻"——基础设施正在逼近阈值，大规模实际应用变得切实可行。瓶颈从"我们能做到吗？"转移到"我们能多快落地？"

对创业者来说，这里有个值得关注的视角：Ginkgo 不只是一家生物科技公司，它是一个平台游戏。他们在构建细胞编程基础设施，让其他公司可以在上面搭建业务——类似合成生物学领域的 AWS。Kelly 认为 AI 是最终让这个平台在经济上可行的解锁钥匙，因为它大幅降低了"设计-构建-测试"循环的成本，而这个循环正是所有生物学工作的核心。

反直觉的洞见：以往技术对生物科技无关紧要的原因，并不是因为沟通或信息管理不重要——而是因为真正的瓶颈从来都是：我们能不能实际设计和操控生物系统。AI 恰恰解决的就是这个瓶颈。
