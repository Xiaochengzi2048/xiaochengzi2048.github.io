① Andrej Karpathy · AI Researcher & Educator

Karpathy 指出 LLM 个性化记忆功能存在一个普遍缺陷：模型会过度放大过去的单次提问，将其误判为用户的长期兴趣并反复提及。他推测这源于训练阶段模型对 context window 内容的过度依赖，导致在推理时出现偏差。

---

Karpathy observes a common LLM personalization flaw: memory features cause models to over-weight past queries, persistently surfacing old topics as major interests. He theorizes this stems from a training-time bias where context window content is heavily weighted for task relevance, which then misfires at inference time.

🎙 Podcast

Biology's Waymo Moment: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly

Ginkgo Bioworks首席执行官杰森·凯利认为，AI对生物技术的变革意义是真实深远的——与以往历次技术浪潮不同——因为它能从根本上改变生物工程的方式，而非仅优化后台流程。凯利于2008年创立公司后艰难自营六年，直到2014年通过山姆·阿尔特曼的博客文章获得联系并加入Y Combinator，才真正获得资本支持。他将生物学类比为唯一可与计算机媲美的"编码"系统，DNA如同可编程代码，但其"编译调试"过程是物理性的，成本高昂，仍是关键瓶颈。有别于Chai Bio、Arc Institute等专注AI驱动生物设计的竞争者，Ginkgo已战略性转向"后端"问题——降低实验室测试成本、加速迭代周期，并与OpenAI展开合作，探索AI模型如何推动这一实验室执行层的变革。

Ginkgo Bioworks CEO Jason Kelly argues that AI represents a genuinely transformative moment for biotechnology—unlike previous tech waves—because it can fundamentally change how biology is engineered, not just optimize back-office processes. Kelly, who bootstrapped the company for six years after founding it in 2008 before connecting with Sam Altman and joining Y Combinator in 2014, has strategically pivoted Ginkgo's focus away from the AI-driven biological design space (where players like Chai Bio and Arc Institute's EVO 2 compete) and toward the "back-end" problem: making lab testing and iteration cycles cheaper and faster. He frames biology as the only other "coded" system besides computers—where DNA serves as programmable code—but notes that the compile-debug cycle is a physical, expensive process that remains a critical bottleneck. Most recently, Ginkgo has partnered with OpenAI to explore how AI models can accelerate this laboratory execution layer, which Kelly believes is a more tractable engineering problem than the science-heavy design challenge.
