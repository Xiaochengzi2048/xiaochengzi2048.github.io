① Andrej Karpathy · I Like To Train Large Deep Neural Nets. Previously Director Of Ai @ Tesla,

Karpathy observes a common flaw in LLM personalization: retrieved memories, even from a single past interaction, can become disproportionately influential and get repeatedly surfaced in unrelated contexts. He hypothesizes this "overfitting to memory" stems from training dynamics, where models learn a bias toward using context-window information heavily, then over-apply that tendency at inference time when memory features RAG irrelevant content into the prompt.

Karpathy 指出 LLM 个性化功能存在一个普遍缺陷：即使只是几个月前的一次对话记录，也可能被模型过度重视，在不相关的场景中反复提及。他推测这种"对记忆的过拟合"源于训练机制——模型在训练时学会了大量依赖 context window 中的信息，导致在推理阶段当 memory 功能通过 RAG 引入无关内容时，模型也会过度套用这一偏好。

⑦ Aaron Levie · Ceo @ Box

Aaron Levie argues that Jevons paradox is playing out in AI, where lower software costs are driving more demand rather than less, enabling companies across industries to tackle projects previously out of reach. He predicts software will permeate every corner of the economy, from marketing automation to life sciences research, and that human engineers will remain essential for prompting, overseeing, and maintaining AI agents. He pushes back on advice to avoid engineering careers, asserting that those who understand software will hold strong economic advantage as the world becomes increasingly software-driven.

Aaron Levie 认为，AI 正在引发"杰文斯悖论"——软件开发成本的降低不但没有减少需求，反而激发了更多需求，让各行各业的公司得以启动以前无力承担的项目。他预测软件将渗透经济的每个角落，从营销自动化到生命科学研究，而人类工程师在提示、监督和维护 AI agent 方面仍将不可或缺。他反驳了"不要学工程"的建议，强调随着世界愈发依赖软件，真正懂软件的人将占据强大的经济优势。

Biology's Waymo Moment: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly

In a surprising historical note, Ginkgo Bioworks CEO Jason Kelly bootstrapped the synthetic biology company for six years after founding it in 2008 before a Sam Altman blog post led to a YC investment in 2014. Kelly argues that unlike previous tech waves — the internet, social media — AI represents the first technology revolution that will genuinely disrupt the fundamentals of biology and biopharma. Ginkgo's core insight is that DNA is essentially code (ATCG instead of 0s and 1s), and the challenge of "programming" biology splits into two problems: designing better DNA sequences (the software-like front end, now increasingly addressed by AI models like Chai Bio and Arc Institute's EVO 2) and making the physical compile-debug lab cycle cheaper and faster (the hardware-driven back end). Kelly has strategically shifted Ginkgo's focus almost entirely to the back-end engineering problem, including a recent project with OpenAI, reasoning that engineering challenges can be "rendered to dust" through sustained effort while pure science problems remain unpredictable. He positions Ginkgo as a rare Silicon Valley-minded actor in a life sciences tools landscape that he says largely lacks the ambition to fundamentally reinvent its own processes.

Ginkgo Bioworks的CEO Jason Kelly披露了一个出人意料的创业历程：公司2008年成立后自力更生长达六年，直到2014年Sam Altman发表博客文章后才通过YC获得融资。Kelly认为，与互联网、社交媒体等历次技术浪潮不同，AI是第一个真正能够从根本上颠覆生物学和生物制药行业的技术革命。Ginkgo的核心洞察在于，DNA本质上就是代码（ATCG而非0和1），"编程"生物学的挑战可以分为两个部分：设计更优质的DNA序列（类似软件的前端，如今AI模型如Chai Bio和Arc Institute的EVO 2正在攻克此问题），以及让实验室的物理编译调试周期更快更廉价（硬件驱动的后端）。Kelly已将Ginkgo的战略重心几乎全部转向后端工程问题，并与OpenAI开展了合作项目，他认为工程挑战可以通过持续投入"彻底攻克"，而纯科学问题则难以预测。他将Ginkgo定位为生命科学工具领域中罕见的具有硅谷思维的力量，批评该行业普遍缺乏从根本上重新发明自身流程的雄心。
