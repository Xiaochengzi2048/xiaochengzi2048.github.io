🎙 Podcast

Can One Person Build a Billion-Dollar Startup? — The General Intelligence Company of New York

纽约通用智能公司（General Intelligence Company）的创始人 Andrew 正致力于实现他所称的"单人十亿美元公司"愿景——即一名创始人通过分层 AI 管理代理体系运营整个企业。他们的首款产品 Co-founder 已吸引逾 8,000 家初创公司用于自动化运营，但团队意识到单纯的助手工具远不足以支撑这一宏大愿景。此次活动上最令人意外的发布是全新产品 Co-founder CTO——一个完全基于云端的自主工程部门，可自动完成功能开发、浏览器 QA 测试、代码部署及基础设施管理。该产品界面灵感来自《星际争霸》《文明》等策略游戏，将代理编排呈现为实时管理画布而非传统聊天界面。公司将自身定位为"中间管理层"，优先集成 Claude Code、Browserbase 等第三方代理，而非从头重建每项能力。

The General Intelligence Company of New York, founded roughly a year ago, is building toward what founder Andrew calls the "one-person billion-dollar company" — a solo operator running an entire business through a hierarchy of AI manager agents coordinating specialized vertical agents below them. Their first product, Co-founder, gained traction with over 8,000 startups using it to automate operations like email drafting and LinkedIn candidate research, but the team recognized an assistant alone couldn't realize their full vision. Their newest and most surprising reveal — debuted live at this event — is Co-founder CTO, described as the first fully autonomous engineering department in the cloud, capable of building features, running browser-based QA tests, deploying code via Vercel, and managing infrastructure without human intervention. The product's interface is notably inspired by strategy video games like Starcraft and Civilization, treating agent orchestration as a real-time management canvas rather than a chat UI. The company positions itself as a "middle management layer," deliberately integrating third-party agents like Claude Code and Browserbase rather than rebuilding every capability from scratch.
