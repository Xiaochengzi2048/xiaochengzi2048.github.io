① Andrej Karpathy · AI Researcher & Educator

Karpathy 指出 LLM 在个性化方面存在一个普遍问题：模型会过度依赖历史对话记录，导致不相关的旧内容反复出现。他推测这源于训练时的偏差——训练阶段的 context 通常与任务高度相关，使模型在推理时养成了过度利用已有信息的习惯。

---

Karpathy observes a common LLM personalization problem where models over-index on past context, causing irrelevant memories to resurface repeatedly. He theorizes this stems from a training-time bias where context is usually relevant, leading models to over-utilize provided information at test time.

Biology's Waymo Moment: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly

Ginkgo Bioworks首席执行官Jason Kelly认为，AI对生物技术而言是真正具有变革意义的时刻——与互联网或社交媒体不同，他将后者对生物医药行业的影响斥为"后台IT杂务"。Kelly回顾了Ginkgo在2014年前艰难自力更生六年的历程，直到Sam Altman的一篇博文促成其加入YC，才真正获得资本支持。最值得关注的是，Kelly已将Ginkgo的战略重心从竞争激烈的生物设计端（Chai Bio、Arc Institute等玩家活跃于此）转向更为艰难却少人问津的"测试"端——即自动化并降低DNA编译调试物理流程的成本。他将此定位为工程问题而非科学问题，相信投入足够资源可将其"彻底攻克"，并已与OpenAI合作探索AI辅助实验室工作流程。

Ginkgo Bioworks CEO Jason Kelly argues that AI represents a genuinely transformative moment for biotechnology—unlike the internet or social media, which he dismisses as "back office IT crap" for the industry. Kelly recounts bootstrapping Ginkgo for six years before a Sam Altman blog post led to a YC stint in 2014 that unlocked real capital. Most notably, Kelly has strategically pivoted Ginkgo away from the trendy "design" side of biological engineering (where players like Chai Bio and Arc Institute compete) to focus on the harder, less glamorous "testing" side—automating and cheapening the physical compile-debug cycle of DNA. He frames this as an engineering problem rather than a science problem, believing it can be "rendered to dust" with enough focused resources, and recently partnered with OpenAI to explore AI-assisted lab workflows.
