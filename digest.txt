① Andrej Karpathy · AI Researcher & Educator

Karpathy observes a common LLM issue where memory/personalization features become counterproductive — a single past question gets over-weighted and repeatedly surfaced as a "deep interest," suggesting models are trained to over-rely on context window information. He speculates this bias toward using given context, useful during training, becomes a distraction at inference time.

Karpathy 指出，LLM 的个性化记忆功能往往适得其反——一个月前随口问的问题，会被模型反复当成"核心兴趣"提及，显得用力过猛。他推测这源于训练时模型养成了过度依赖 context window 信息的偏好，在推理阶段反而成了干扰。

Biology's Waymo Moment: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly

Ginkgo Bioworks CEO Jason Kelly argues that AI represents a genuinely transformative moment for biotechnology — unlike the internet or social media, which he dismisses as "back office IT crap" for the industry. Kelly, who bootstrapped the company for six years after founding it straight out of grad school in 2008, describes Ginkgo's core mission as making biology easier to engineer by treating DNA as programmable code. Most notably, Kelly has strategically pivoted away from the AI-driven "design" side of bioengineering (where competitors like Chai Bio and Arc Institute's EVO 2 are active) to focus instead on the physical "testing" infrastructure — making lab compilation and debugging cycles cheaper and faster, including a recent collaboration with OpenAI. He frames this as an engineering problem rather than a science problem, believing it is more predictable and ultimately "renderable to dust" with sufficient resources.

Ginkgo Bioworks首席执行官Jason Kelly认为，AI对生物技术而言代表着一个真正具有变革性的时刻——不同于互联网或社交媒体，他将后者对生物技术行业的影响斥为"后台IT杂务"。Kelly于2008年从研究生院毕业后直接创立公司，在获得融资前靠政府补贴和服务业务艰难维持了六年。值得关注的是，他已将战略重心从AI驱动的生物设计端（Chai Bio和Arc Institute的EVO 2等竞争者活跃于此）转向物理"测试"基础设施，致力于降低实验室编译与调试周期的成本、提升速度，并已与OpenAI开展合作。他将此定位为工程问题而非科学问题，认为这更具可预测性，只要投入足够资源，终将彻底攻克。
