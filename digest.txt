① Andrej Karpathy · AI researcher, ex-Director of AI @ Tesla / ex-Founding Team @ OpenAI

LLM personalization has a subtle but real problem: memory systems are too eager to latch on. A single question asked two months ago can turn into a permanent "deep interest" that keeps surfacing — models try too hard to use whatever context they have. Karpathy notes this bias likely originates in training, where most context is relevant to the task, creating an overgeneralization at inference time.

LLM 的个性化记忆功能有个微妙但真实的问题：模型太爱"抓住"每条记忆不放。两个月前问过的一个问题，可能被模型永久标注为你的"深度兴趣"，反复出现在回复里——这种"努力过头"的感觉令人不适。Karpathy 认为这种偏差很可能源于训练阶段：训练时上下文几乎都和任务相关，导致模型在推理时产生了过度泛化。

Jevons paradox is playing out in software right now: as AI makes software cheaper to build, companies aren't cutting their software spend — they're expanding their ambitions. Businesses that previously couldn't afford to tackle certain software projects are now going for them. Levie argues we're entering a phase where software gets used for entirely new categories of work, not just as a cost-reducer.

软件领域正在上演 Jevons 悖论：AI 让软件开发成本下降，企业不是减少投入，而是在扩大野心。原来那些因为价格望而却步的软件项目，现在都被提上日程。Levie 认为我们正进入一个新阶段——软件不只是用来降本，而是开始渗透到全新的业务场景。

当 AI agent 让"加功能"变得轻而易举，设计师的角色就从执行者变成了判断者。工作不再是推敲像素，而是决定什么应该存在、各部分如何协作、如何让人保持掌控感、以及如何让智能显得可信赖。Ryo 说，品味和判断力一直是最难的那部分——现在它们是唯一重要的部分。

Biology's Waymo Moment: Ginkgo Bioworks' Jason Kelly
Training Data

Jason Kelly, founder and CEO of Ginkgo Bioworks, has spent 18 years trying to make biology programmable. His company just ran an experiment with OpenAI that he believes is the most important result in AI-driven science to date: they gave a reasoning model control of a robotic lab and tasked it with optimizing a protein synthesis process. After six rounds of 384-well-plate experiments — about 30,000 total runs — the model beat Stanford's published state-of-the-art benchmark by 40%. The model wasn't smarter than the scientists. It won because it could run experiments 24/7 and design each next round of tests based on the data from the last.

Kelly's "Waymo moment" analogy is precise: for decades, lab automation was like a subway — high automation, zero flexibility, only useful if your experiment fit the exact route. AI is finally enabling the autonomous car version of the lab: full flexibility, full automation. His vision for what comes next is striking — imagine 100 AI scientists simultaneously pursuing different Alzheimer's hypotheses in one shared robotic lab, sharing raw experimental data with each other *daily* rather than publishing papers every two years. The information sharing advantage alone, he argues, would beat human labs even if the AI were less intelligent.

The economics are equally disruptive. Today, less than 5% of research spending goes to actual reagents — the real cost is lab space, equipment, and people. An autonomous lab flips this ratio toward 90% reagent spend, meaning roughly 10x more data per dollar. Kelly is already selling this vision to the Department of Energy as part of Project Genesis, a White House initiative to double the speed of scientific discovery. His urgency is geopolitical too: three years ago, less than 5% of new drug discoveries came from China. Last quarter, it was 40%.

"All of the previous revolutions in tech have been totally meaningless to biotechnology and biopharma. Not this. This is actually going to change the fundamentals of how we do science."

Jason Kelly 是 Ginkgo Bioworks 的创始人兼 CEO，他花了 18 年时间试图让生物学变得可编程。他的公司刚刚与 OpenAI 合作完成了一项实验，他认为这是迄今为止 AI 驱动科学领域最重要的成果：他们让一个推理模型控制机器人实验室，优化一个蛋白质合成流程。经过六轮共约 3 万次 384 孔板实验，模型将斯坦福发布的最优标杆提升了 40%。模型并不比科学家更聪明——它赢在可以 7×24 小时运行实验，并根据上一轮数据设计下一轮测试。

Kelly 用"Waymo 时刻"来类比实验室自动化的演进：过去几十年，实验室自动化就像地铁——自动化程度高，但零灵活性，只有当你的实验恰好符合既定路线才有用。AI 正在让实验室进入"自动驾驶汽车"时代：既有充分的灵活性，又有完全的自动化。他对未来的设想令人震撼——想象 100 个 AI 科学家在同一个机器人实验室里同时追踪不同的阿尔茨海默症假说，每天互相共享原始实验数据，而不是每两年发一篇论文。他认为，仅凭信息共享这一个优势，AI 实验室就能击败人类实验室——即便 AI 本身并不更聪明。

经济账同样颠覆性。如今，研究经费中不到 5% 用于实际的试剂——真正的成本是实验室空间、仪器和人力。自动化实验室将把这个比例翻转为约 90% 用于试剂，意味着每一美元能产出约 10 倍的数据量。Kelly 已经在向美国能源部推销这个愿景，作为白宫"创世纪计划"的一部分——目标是在未来几年将科学发现的速度提升一倍。他的紧迫感也有地缘政治背景：三年前，中国的新药发现不到全球的 5%；上个季度，这个数字已经是 40%。

"过去所有的技术革命对生物技术和生物制药来说都毫无意义。但这一次不同。这将从根本上改变我们做科学的方式。"
